04 · Projects

Selected Work

SmartLink — Context-Aware URL Shortener & Routing Engine — 2026
A full-stack link router that decides where traffic goes based on device, location, referrer, and time.
▶
Problem
Most URL shorteners just redirect. Real traffic routing needs to account for who's clicking, from where, and when — without adding noticeable latency.
Approach
Built a full-stack system with FastAPI, React, PostgreSQL, and Redis, centered on a custom priority-ordered rule engine that routes traffic by device, geolocation, referrer, and time of day. Used Redis caching to keep redirects fast, and added custom sliding-window rate limiting and A/B traffic splitting on top.
Result
Sub-10ms redirects in production, plus a real-time analytics dashboard for tracking traffic patterns and split-test performance.

Tactical Football Analysis Pipeline — 2026
Tracking players and extracting tactical patterns from raw match footage.
▶
Problem
Match footage is dense and hard to analyze tactically by eye alone — coaches and analysts need structured player and ball positions, not just video.
Approach
Built a detection and tracking pipeline using YOLO11 for player/ball detection and ByteTrack for consistent multi-object tracking across frames, then translated raw tracks into positional and tactical summaries.

AyurSanvaad — Multilingual Consultation Chatbot — 2024–2025
A retrieval-backed chatbot delivering Ayurvedic guidance in 20+ Indian languages.
▶
Problem
Reliable Ayurvedic guidance is hard to access for people who don't speak English or Hindi, and generic chat models hallucinate on domain-specific questions.
Approach
Built a retrieval pipeline pairing Llama with a ChromaDB vector store, grounding responses in a curated knowledge base rather than the model's raw output, and tuned it to hold up across 20+ languages.
Result
Handled 500+ queries a day in production, improved response relevance by 25% over the baseline, and became the basis of a peer-reviewed paper at ICLI 2025.

05 · Process

How I Think

01
Start with the ambiguous ask
Most real problems arrive vague — "help our team understand this data." I spend time turning that into a concrete, testable workflow before writing code.
02
Build for the person who isn't technical
A lot of what I build is used by people who don't want to think about how it works. If it needs a manual, I've usually designed it wrong.
03
Instrument before you optimize
Latency and accuracy numbers I cite in my projects came from actually measuring, not guessing — I'd rather know where time is going than assume.
04
Document as you go
Writing things down while building — not after — is the difference between a teammate ramping up in a day versus a week.
